## Key Excerpts

> "Prompts are no longer casual inputs. They are operational assets. And like any asset that affects outcomes, risk, and compliance, they need structure."

> "A prompt governance framework focuses on three pillars: (1) Tracking prompt changes, (2) Enterprise prompt control, (3) Prompt audits and traceability."

> "Without prompt versioning, teams face issues like: Different teams using different prompt drafts; No rollback option when outputs suddenly degrade; No record of why a prompt was changed in the first place."

> "Enterprise prompt control defines: Who can create prompts; Who can edit existing prompts; Who can approve prompts for production; Who can only view or reuse prompts."

> "A strong prompt governance framework gives organizations visibility, accountability, and confidence, especially when AI outputs impact real people and real decisions."

> "Prompt audits are not about blame. They are about traceability. When AI systems are explainable, organizations can respond faster, correct issues, and demonstrate responsibility."

> "AI systems rarely fail loudly. They drift. A missing line, an overwritten prompt, or an undocumented change can slowly throw outputs off course."
