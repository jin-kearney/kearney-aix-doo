## Key Excerpts

> "PromptOps is a systematic methodology for optimizing the way large language models (LLMs) are prompted... It aims to standardize the process of designing, testing, deploying, refining and storing prompts within an organization. That means systematically managing AI prompts at scale."

> "Prompt drift: Generative AI models are being constantly updated and refined... prompts that were previously performing optimally may no longer do so. Furthermore, because LLMs are non-deterministic, the same input does not always garner the same result. Therefore, engineers must constantly monitor and adjust prompts."

> "Before PromptOps is implemented, an organization typically has prompts scattered across multiple teams and tools, with no structured management in place."

> "Versioning is a standard practice in coding, and it is an essential part of unifying prompting within an organization. Each version of a prompt is given a unique identifier. This enables engineers to track each individual version, allowing them to compare the performance of different prompts and manage prompt drift."

> "Taxonomy development refers to the categorization of prompts using a clear and consistent set of labels and words."

> "Incorporating PromptOps enables organizations to move from a chaotic, ad hoc approach to a coherent and strategic one. The goal is to deliver artificial intelligence tools that perform consistently and effectively."

> "2024 was a breakthrough year for generative AI in terms of adoption. Weekly usage in companies grew from 37% to 72%... there was a 130% increase in spending."
