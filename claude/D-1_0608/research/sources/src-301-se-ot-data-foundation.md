## Key Excerpts

> "The missing link is not more analytics, but the infrastructure and approach required to make OT data usable beyond its original purpose. As a result, many industrial organizations are effectively data-rich and insight-poor: surrounded by operational data, yet unable to use it consistently at scale."

> "OT data is continuous, heterogeneous, and often produced by assets that were never designed to broadly share information."

> "Much of the OT data collected today was designed for compliance, traceability, or basic monitoring, not for optimization. Compliance-grade data answers questions such as whether a process stayed within acceptable limits or whether a batch met regulatory requirements. Analytics-grade data, by contrast, must support continuous analysis, comparison, and improvement. It requires different sampling rates, structures, and contextual information."

> "A common rule of thumb holds that 80% of an AI project is spent on data preparation. In that sense, AI is the finale, not the opening act. It magnifies existing data foundations; if OT data is not consistently modeled and exposed, AI becomes fragile, expensive, and difficult to scale."

> "OT data readiness depends on at least four elements: 1) Data availability, 2) Data access, 3) Data structuration and contextualization, 4) Data publishing."

> "As OT data pipelines expand (sensors and controllers at the edge, analytics, AI cloud applications), the integrity of that data becomes just as critical as its availability."
