## Key Excerpts (from search result summary)

> "Sensor malfunctions can throw off AI predictions, such as a temperature sensor that's out of calibration or a vibration monitor that works intermittently. Predictive maintenance relies heavily on high-fidelity data, and if sensor data is noisy, inconsistent, or incomplete, AI models can't accurately predict failures."

> "Poor data quality creates two major problems: 'false positives' occur when the AI predicts a failure that doesn't actually happen, and 'false negatives' happen when the system misses real warning signs, resulting in unexpected breakdowns."

> "Real-time data synchronization is a sticking point in predictive maintenance systems. Data format inconsistencies further complicate integration, as equipment data might be stored in one format in the CMMS, while IoT sensor data uses another."

> "For equipment utilizing multiple sensors, the complete loss of data from a single sensor significantly diminishes the predictive maintenance capability of AI models."
