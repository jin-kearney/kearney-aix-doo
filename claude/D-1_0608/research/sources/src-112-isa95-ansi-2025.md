## Key Excerpts (from search result)

ISA-95 (ANSI/ISA-95 / IEC 62264) is the global standard that defines how business systems and the shop floor exchange information. The International Society of Automation (ISA) developed ISA-95 to create an abstract model for information exchange among manufacturing control functions and business functions in an enterprise.

The 2025 version (ANSI/ISA 95.00.01-2025) enhances IT/OT convergence for industrial automation.

**ISA-95 defines:**
- Five hierarchical levels (Level 0–4)
- Four operational domains: production, quality, maintenance, inventory
- Data objects that move between ERP and MES

**IT/OT Convergence definition:**
Integrating information technology (IT) systems with operational technology (OT) systems — IT systems manage data and business, OT systems control physical industrial processes. IT/OT convergence breaks down silos, improves visibility, enhances security, and drives innovation by enabling data-driven decisions.

Level 3 is the critical bridge between OT and IT in the ISA-95 model.

**Implementation Technologies:**
- B2MML: XML data model for ERP↔MES communication
- OPC UA: real-time OT/IT communication
