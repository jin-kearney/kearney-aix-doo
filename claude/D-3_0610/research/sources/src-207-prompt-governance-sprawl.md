## Relevant Excerpt

### The Prompt Sprawl Problem
"Prompt sprawl has become a notable challenge as organizations increasingly adopt AI tools, occurring when a large number of prompts are created without adequate oversight, leading to confusion and inefficiencies."

"Teams are using GenAI daily, often productively but prompts live everywhere: personal chat threads, shared documents, Slack messages, wiki pages."

### Consequences of Uncontrolled Sprawl
- Inconsistency in the outputs generated by AI models
- Increased operational costs as teams navigate numerous redundant or conflicting prompts
- Prompt sprawl creates reliability issues, inefficiency and risk long before leadership realizes what's happening
- Impedes team collaboration, with individuals adopting varied methods

### Governance Gap
According to Deloitte: 84% of companies have not redesigned roles around AI, and only 21% have a mature AI-agent governance model, while approximately 75% plan to deploy agentic AI within two years — a gap between deployment velocity and governance maturity.

### EU AI Act Impact (from prompt governance search results)
The EU AI Act's January 2025 implementation requires documented governance for business-critical AI prompts, affecting 41% of enterprise prompt libraries (according to PwC's March 2025 compliance analysis).

### Governance Pillars
1. **Centralized repositories**: Best practices recommend creating folders with each prompt as a single code file in a versioning system; most prompts remain scattered across individual workflows
2. **Quality standards**: Most organizations lack systematic approaches to prompt optimization
3. **Performance measurement**: Most organizations operate without baseline metrics
4. **Security frameworks**: SOC 2 Type II, HIPAA, GDPR compliance
5. **Automated lifecycle governance**: Enforcing version control and compliance, reducing operational risks

### Access Control at Scale
"A scalable system must define who can edit prompts, who can run heavy simulations, and who can promote versions to production."

### IBM Research Finding (2025)
IBM's Thomas Watson Research Center found in March 2025 that 68% of commonly used prompt templates unintentionally introduce demographic bias — underscoring the need for governance review processes.
