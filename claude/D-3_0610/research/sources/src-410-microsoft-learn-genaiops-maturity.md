## Excerpt

Official Microsoft Learn documentation, last updated 2025-11-18.

### Assessment scores (from linked GenAIOps Maturity Model Assessment)

| Level | Label | Score range | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | Initial | 0–9 | Exploring LLM capabilities; no structured practices; no systematic approaches |
| 2 | Defined | 10–14 | Systematizing LLM operations; structured development; room for more sophisticated integration |
| 3 | Managed | 15–19 | Managing advanced LLM workflows with proactive monitoring and structured deployment strategies; "close to achieving operational excellence" |
| 4 | Optimized | 20–28 | Demonstrates operational excellence; sophisticated approach to development, deployment, and monitoring |

### Level 1 → Level 2 advancement actions (prompt-specific)
- "Start experimenting with structured prompt design and basic prompt engineering"
- "Introduce basic metrics for LLM application performance evaluation"

### Level 2 → Level 3 advancement actions (prompt-specific)
- "Develop more complex prompts and integrate them effectively into applications"
- "Implement a systematic approach for LLM application deployment, possibly exploring CI/CD integration"
- "Begin employing more advanced evaluation metrics like groundedness, relevance, and similarity"

### Level 3 → Level 4 advancement actions (prompt-specific)
- "Strengthen your asset management strategies through advanced version control and rollback capabilities"
- "Enhance your monitoring strategies with predictive analytics and comprehensive content safety measures"

### Key quote
"Use the GenAIOps Maturity Model Assessment to determine your organization's current GenAIOps maturity level. The questionnaire helps you understand your organization's current capabilities and identify areas for improvement."

### Implication for D-3 roadmap
The 0–9/10–14/15–19/20–28 score bands provide an **assessment-backed staging** (not just descriptive labels). Organizations can benchmark themselves before starting a prompt/harness asset-ization program. Level 3 ("managed") explicitly calls out "asset management strategies through advanced version control" — this is the formal Microsoft confirmation that prompt asset-ization is a Level 3 capability.
